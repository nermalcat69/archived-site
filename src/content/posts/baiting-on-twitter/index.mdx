## The Concept

Reverse Proxy Mapped to Localhost

### The Recording Process

Participants documented:

- **Morning sounds** (6-10 AM)
- _Afternoon ambiance_ (12-4 PM)
- **Evening atmosphere** (6-10 PM)

The recordings captured everything from construction noise to children playing, from street musicians to subway rumbles.

## Creating the Sound Map

Using the collected audio and location data, I created an interactive map where visitors could:

1. **Click any location** to hear its acoustic signature
2. _Compare sounds_ across different times of day
3. **Discover sonic patterns** unique to each neighborhood

### Unexpected Discoveries

The project revealed fascinating _acoustic territories_:

- **Coffee shops** created consistent morning soundscapes
- _Construction sites_ moved predictably through the area
- **Evening sounds** varied dramatically by block
- _Weekend patterns_ differed significantly from weekdays

## Community Impact

The sound map became a **living document** of neighborhood character. Participants reported:

- Increased _awareness of their acoustic environment_
- **New appreciation** for everyday sounds
- _Stronger sense of place_ and community identity
- **Desire to preserve** certain neighborhood sounds

## Technical Implementation

The project used:

- Custom recording app with GPS tagging
- Audio processing for noise reduction
- Interactive web map with playback controls
- _Time-based filtering_ for different listening experiences

## Reflection

This experiment transformed how participants _listened to their environment_. By making the invisible visible, we created a new form of **community documentation**. The sound map became not just an artistic project, but a tool for _urban planning_ and **neighborhood advocacy**.

The most powerful outcome was hearing participants say: _"I never noticed how many birds live here until I started really listening."_
